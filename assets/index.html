About Me

I'm a Santa Clara University graduate with a B.A. in Film and a minor in Perfoming Arts that has somehow ended up fascinated by technology and computer science. Post-graduation adulting has been a whirlwind of growth and challenges. I moved out of my mother's house and landed my first job at a Mexican Grill restaurant. My mom wasn't too happy about that even though I hooked her up with delicious shrimp tacos. At 22, I landed a job at a trucking company where I negotiated rates with import/export clients and made bonuses off of the marginal profits. All this without a background in finance or accounting. So what happened with my film degree? After a year there, I began working for a subtitling company called SDI (Subtilting and Dubbing International.) That was my foot in the door! I met people from all over the world. I learned how to create subtitle files from scratch, how to edit and sync them to different video frame rates and finally dove into the industry I wanted to be in. This helped me land a 3-month gig at Netflix, which then got extended to 4 months, 6 months, 8, and finally 12 months. (Yes, I'm that good. Hire me? :P) Then, they let me go. It was a tough breakup. I wanted to get married (be full-time, not a contactor), they just wanted to be friends with benefits at the time (cool benefits by the way.) So now what? You live and you learn and you grow! I started looking into machine-assisted translation, localization engineering, computational linguistics, and although I'm not sure how I'm going to get into these roles, I knew I had to take this course. So here I am, excited to see where I'll be towards the end of 2019.
